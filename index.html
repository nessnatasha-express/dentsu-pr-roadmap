Routing Rubric

Every brief enters through a single triage layer regardless of operating model. This structure is what the routing and resourcing milestones in each phase are implementing in software.
Intelligence Layer
  • All briefs enter here first for triage, with asset reuse and duplication checks before work begins.
  • Briefs informed for cultural consultation, media strategy, and regional business considerations.
  • Adaptation, scale, and localisation always route to offshore hubs.
  • Specialty overflow goes to the global creative network.
BU Studios
  • Primary assets and global toolkits.
  • London: Chivas, Ballantine's, Glenlivet, Royal Salute
  • Paris: Martell, Mumm, Lillet, Havana Club
  • Stockholm: Absolut, Malibu, Kahlúa, HoT
  • Dublin: Jameson, Redbreast, Powers, Midleton
Centres of Excellence
  • Shoot production, packaging design, POS design, e-commerce, GTR, UX design.
  • Dedicated creative and production experts flex across all BUs to support campaign toolkits.
  • Offshore production network supports efficiency and informed business affairs.
ME Studios
  • Activations, social, local strategy across NOAM, LATAM, S. Europe, AME, APAC, China, GTR, N. Europe, India, NADL.
  • Small nimble teams manage local activation and in-market execution.
  • All adaptation and versioning traffic passes to offshore hubs.
Offshore Hubs
  • Localised, hygiene, and secondary work.
  • India: global/regional adaptation hub, all tiers.
  • Poland: CEE adaptation and versioning, Europe markets.
  • Brazil: LATAM regional hub for adaptation and production.
Flex Pool / Shared Production
  • Cross-tier surge capacity for specialty, bespoke, or unusual briefs.
  • Shared production for peaks, accessed by any tier without permanent headcount.
  • Subject to rate card.

Reading the comparison

Wrike is the strongest alternative of the three new entrants. It is built for the same governance-heavy, multi-stakeholder approval use case as Workfront, with mature request-intake forms, workload management, and an existing Adobe Creative Cloud integration. It is also the only one of the three with a credible answer to PR's resource-allocation and capacity-planning requirements. It still has no DAM, no Bynder connector, and no documented reversibility/decoupling framework, so the eligibility-pillar gaps remain.

monday.com and ClickUp remain the lowest-cost options and now show a real AI advantage: both ship agentic AI platforms (AI Work Platform, ClickUp Brain) with drafting and enterprise search built in, which Workfront still handles through GENIE rather than natively. Neither has a DAM/MAM layer or a track record against PR's eligibility pillars (reuse enforcement, reversibility, multi-partner tenant isolation).

On the forward integration roadmap (BI, Bynder, Figma, the Google brief generator), no platform in this set has a confirmed, documented connector for Bynder or Figma specifically. Workfront's Bynder link is the only one named in submitted RFP material, and it is built rather than out-of-the-box. A Figma connector and a Google-based brief generator hookup would be custom API work for all four platforms; none should be assumed "integration-ready" on that basis alone.
